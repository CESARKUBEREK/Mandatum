Layer-by-layer breakdown
01
Middleware · Router
Country Router
What rules apply here?
›
What it does
Reads only the first pages of the document. Detects the country, legal framework (e.g. Ley 19.886 Chile, FAR USA, LCSP Spain), and language. Routes to the correct jurisdiction-specific intelligence.
This is the middleware layer — it ensures that Chilean procurement rules are never applied to a Mexican tender, and that LATAM-specific obligations (fianza, garantía de seriedad) are correctly recognised.
Detects
Country and jurisdiction
Legal framework (national procurement law)
Language (ES / PT / EN / FR)
Portal of origin (ChileCompra, CompraNet, SECOP…)
Tender type (obras, servicios, suministros)
Output
set_prompts → cl / co / mx / es / generic
Legal framework identifier
Language flag for downstream layers
Jurisdiction risk profile
02
Task 01 · Extraction
Structured Extraction
What does the document say?
›
What it does
Converts the entire tender document into a machine-readable JSON — a "digital twin of the pliego". Extracts every fact without interpretation. Ultra-rigid: invents nothing, normalises everything.
Dates are output as ISO 8601. Amounts are numeric. Bases administrativas take precedence over the ficha resumen when conflicts exist.
Extracts
All dates and deadlines (ISO normalised)
Amounts, currencies, budget lines
Guarantees and bonds (fianzas, garantías)
Technical and administrative requirements
Penalties and fine structures
Evaluation criteria and weightings
Contract conditions and special clauses
Output
Structured JSON — digital twin of the tender
Feeds → Compliance Matrix (Task 02)
Feeds → Risk Engine (Task 03)
Conflict resolution log (bases vs ficha)
03
Task 02 · Compliance
Compliance Matrix
Can we comply?
›
What it does
Transforms the JSON from Layer 02 into an exhaustive, actionable requirement inventory. Goes beyond the explicit — surfaces hidden obligations, legally-implied requirements, and operational constraints that are never written but always enforced.
This is the internal execution machine of the bid. Each requirement is tagged with who owns it, when it must be fulfilled, and what happens if it is missed.
Builds
RT-nn (Technical requirements)
RA-nn (Administrative requirements)
RF-nn (Financial requirements)
Hidden and implicit legal obligations
Eliminatory vs weighted requirements
Owner assignment (legal / finance / ops)
Each requirement includes
Eliminatory flag (yes/no)
Responsible team (legal, finance, ops)
Fulfilment deadline
Risk if not met (severity)
Feeds → Risk Engine (Task 03)
04
Task 03 · Decision
Risk Engine
Should we bid?
›
What it does
Synthesises all upstream data — structured JSON, full compliance matrix, and original document — against a 42-category risk framework. Produces concrete, non-generic risk assessments with severities, probabilities, and actionable mitigations.
The output directly answers the executive question. Not "there is risk" — but "this specific clause creates this specific exposure under this legal framework, and here is what you must do about it."
42-category framework covers
Legal and contractual risk
Financial and pricing risk
Operational delivery risk
Regulatory and compliance risk
Intellectual property exposure
Implicit legal risks (the dangerous ones)
Missing documents (critical flag)
Produces
Concrete risk list (severity + probability)
Actionable mitigations per risk
7 strategic Go/No-Go alerts
Missing documents — critical list
Implicit legal risk analysis

The power is in the combination

Each layer alone is useful.
Together they create something that doesn't exist anywhere else.

Layer 02 without Layer 03
Data without executability. You know what it says — not what you must do.
Layer 03 without Layer 04
A checklist without strategy. You know what to comply with — not whether you should try.
Layer 04 without Layer 02–03
Opinions without evidence. Risk assessments built on incomplete data are dangerous.
