Three principles that govern everything
Principle 01
Your data never crosses our boundary.
Gates II and III — where your pricing, margins, walk-away conditions, and final contracts live — run inside an isolated data plane. That plane has no connection to our intelligence layer. This is an architectural separation, not a policy.
Verifiable: Network traffic analysis and a full SBOM audit confirm zero egress from the Gate II / III data plane.
Principle 02
You hold the keys. Always.
Bring Your Own Key encryption means your data is encrypted with keys that live in your KMS — AWS KMS, Azure Key Vault, or GCP Cloud KMS. Mandatum cannot decrypt your data even if compelled. Revoking your key deletes everything, immediately.
Verifiable: FIPS 140-3 HSM-backed KMS audit logs give your team real-time visibility into every key access attempt.
Principle 03
Your SOC sees everything. We see nothing.
Every event — every document accessed, every query run, every login — is exported via OpenTelemetry to your SIEM. Your security team has full visibility without depending on Mandatum to surface it. The logs belong to you.
Verifiable: Immutable log chain with custody verification. Compatible with Splunk, Microsoft Sentinel, Chronicle, and Elastic.

How the intelligence layer learns — without seeing your data
What is Differential Privacy?
A blindfolded audit. The system learns from the aggregate. It learns nothing about you.

When Gate I signals contribute to our shared intelligence layer, they do so through a mathematical mechanism called Differential Privacy. It adds precisely calibrated noise to the data before it leaves your tenant. The result: the intelligence layer learns patterns across thousands of tenders — but cannot determine whether your company participated in any given round, or reconstruct any of your decisions.

This is a mathematical guarantee, not a contractual promise. The proof is auditable by your security team or any independent third party. The ε value — the privacy budget — is a number, not a policy document.

Gate I signals
ε = 1.0
20% of risk threshold — strong privacy guarantee
An attacker with full access to our servers cannot determine if your company participated in a given round. Your strategic decisions are indistinguishable from noise.
Gates II & III — pricing, mandates, contracts
No ε needed
These data points never enter the intelligence layer
Pricing, margins, mandates, and contracts require no privacy budget because they never move. The protection is architectural isolation — not noise, not policy, not a promise.

Questions your security team will ask — answered directly
Question
Was your model trained on our pricing or contract data?
Answer
No. The intelligence layer only receives anonymized Gate I signals and optional differentially private gradients. With ε ≤ 1.0, reconstructing any source data is mathematically infeasible. This is independently verifiable — not a promise we ask you to trust.
Question
Who holds the encryption keys?
Answer
You do. Always. BYOK means your keys live in your KMS. Mandatum never has access to keys in plaintext. If you revoke your master key, every byte of your data becomes permanently unreadable — including to us. Crypto-shredding is instantaneous.
Question
What happens to our data if we leave Mandatum?
Answer
You export everything in open formats. We execute certified crypto-shredding of all copies within 30 days. For Air-Gapped deployments, the software continues to operate indefinitely without connectivity — your historical decision mandates remain accessible on your infrastructure.
Question
Can we opt out of contributing to your intelligence layer?
Answer
Yes. In every tier. Opt-out is a right, not a penalty clause. You retain all core Mandatum functionality. You forfeit only comparative benchmarks. For Air-Gapped, zero-connectivity mode is the default — participation requires explicit opt-in per export.
Question
Can your team access our data for support or debugging?
Answer
No standing access. Any support access requires JIT provisioning approved by your IAM team, is time-limited, fully logged, and exported to your SIEM. Mandatum cannot grant itself access to your tenant. Your team controls the approval workflow entirely.
