The problem

Most companies don't lose because they lack effort. They lose because they make the wrong decisions at the wrong time.

Capital is finite. Engineering capacity is finite. Legal attention is finite. Every mis-directed pursuit is a cost that cannot be recovered.

01
Pursue weak opportunities
Teams chase tenders with no realistic path to win, burning engineering, legal, and finance capacity on bids that were never winnable.
02
Commit talent too early
Scarce technical resources are deployed before strategic fit and compliance burden have been assessed — before leadership has decided to bid.
03
Submit offers with hidden risk
Pricing errors, unchecked deviations, and unreviewed legal clauses destroy margin at the moment of submission — when it's already too late.
04
Sign contracts that erode margin
Negotiation creep turns revenue into loss. Unauthorized concessions are made under pressure, without executive visibility or approved boundaries.
